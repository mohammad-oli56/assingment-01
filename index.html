" Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, is globally recognized as the "Father of Microfinance." He pioneered the concept of microcredit and microfinance through the Grameen Bank, providing small loans to impoverished individuals without requiring collateral. His groundbreaking work has empowered millions, especially women, to rise above poverty and achieve financial independence. In 2006, he and the Grameen Bank were awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. His leadership as the Chief Adviser of Bangladesh is remembered for fostering sustainable development and inspiring global change."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus
